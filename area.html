Aryabhata's Area of Triangle

In Ganitapada 6, Aryabhata gives the area of a triangle and wrote, ”Tribhujasya phalashriram samadalakoti bhujardhasamvargah” that translates to,

”for a triangle, the result of a perpendicular with the half-side is the area.”

Modern method of Aryabhata’s Area of Triangle:

Area ABC = 12·Base ×Altitude

The area of a triangle is the product of 12 of one side and the perpendicular (from the opposite vertex) to it.

i.e, Area ABC=12·AC ×BC
